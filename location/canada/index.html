Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Canada in?

Canada uses UTC-08:00, UTC-07:00, UTC-06:00, UTC-05:00, UTC-04:00, and UTC-03:30. Based on its capital, Ottawa, this is 5 hours behind London (GMT/BST) and the same time as New York (EST/EDT). Note that Canada spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Ottawa.

Does Canada observe Daylight Saving Time?

Most of Canada observes Daylight Saving Time from March to November, with the exception of most of Saskatchewan. This affects how far ahead or behind Canada is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Canada use?

Canada uses the Canadian Dollar (CAD, symbol $). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Canada?

The official language of Canada is English/French. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Canada?

June to September for warm weather and accessible national parks, or December to March for winter sports. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Canada, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit Canada?

citizens of the US can enter with just a passport; many other visa-exempt nationalities including the UK, EU and Australia need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) for air travel; others require a visitor visa. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with Canada's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is Canada known for?

Canada is known for Banff and the Rocky Mountains, Niagara Falls, and multicultural cities like Toronto and Vancouver. It's located in Americas (North America).
