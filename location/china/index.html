Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is China in?

China uses UTC+08:00. Based on its capital, Beijing, this is 7 hours ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 12 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does China observe Daylight Saving Time?

China uses a single time zone nationwide and has not observed Daylight Saving Time since 1991. This affects how far ahead or behind China is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does China use?

China uses the Chinese Yuan (CNY, symbol ¥). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in China?

The official language of China is Mandarin. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit China?

April to May or September to October when temperatures are mild and skies are typically clearer. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in China, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit China?

most foreign visitors need a visa arranged in advance through a Chinese embassy or consulate, though some nationalities qualify for visa-free transit of up to 144 hours in select cities. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with China's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is China known for?

China is known for the Great Wall, the Forbidden City, high-speed rail, and a fast-growing modern skyline alongside ancient history. It's located in Asia (Eastern Asia).
