Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is South Korea in?

South Korea uses UTC+09:00. Based on its capital, Seoul, this is 8 hours ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 13 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does South Korea observe Daylight Saving Time?

South Korea does not observe Daylight Saving Time and keeps a fixed UTC+09:00 offset year-round. This affects how far ahead or behind South Korea is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does South Korea use?

South Korea uses the South Korean Won (KRW, symbol ₩). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in South Korea?

The official language of South Korea is Korean. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit South Korea?

March to May or September to November for mild weather and seasonal scenery like cherry blossoms or autumn leaves. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in South Korea, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.
