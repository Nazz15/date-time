About Brazil

Brazil spans 4 timezone(s) (UTC-05:00, UTC-04:00, UTC-03:00, and UTC-02:00), with its capital Brasília located in Americas. The official currency is the Brazilian Real (BRL) and the primary language is Portuguese. Brazil's international dial code is +55.

Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Brazil in?

Brazil uses UTC-05:00, UTC-04:00, UTC-03:00, and UTC-02:00. Based on its capital, Brasília, this is 4 hours behind London (GMT/BST) and 1 hour ahead of New York (EST/EDT). Note that Brazil spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Brasília.

Does Brazil observe Daylight Saving Time?

Brazil does not currently observe Daylight Saving Time nationwide, having suspended the practice in 2019. This affects how far ahead or behind Brazil is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Brazil use?

Brazil uses the Brazilian Real (BRL, symbol R$). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Brazil?

The official language of Brazil is Portuguese. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Brazil?

May to October for the dry season in most regions, while Rio's Carnival typically falls in February or March. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Brazil, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

What is Brazil known for?

Brazil is known for Rio de Janeiro's Carnival and Christ the Redeemer, the Amazon rainforest, and Iguazu Falls. It's located in Americas (South America).
