Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Australia in?

Australia uses UTC+08:00, UTC+09:30, and UTC+10:00. Based on its capital, Canberra, this is 9 hours ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 14 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT). Note that Australia spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Canberra.

Does Australia observe Daylight Saving Time?

Daylight Saving Time is observed in the southeastern states from October to April; Queensland, the Northern Territory and Western Australia do not change clocks. This affects how far ahead or behind Australia is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Australia use?

Australia uses the Australian Dollar (AUD, symbol $). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Australia?

The official language of Australia is English. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Australia?

September to November or March to May for mild weather across most regions, avoiding the most extreme summer heat. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Australia, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit Australia?

most visitors need an Electronic Travel Authority (ETA) or eVisitor visa applied for online before arrival, available to a wide range of nationalities including the US, UK, EU and Canada. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with Australia's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is Australia known for?

Australia is known for the Great Barrier Reef, Sydney Opera House, the Outback, and a laid-back coastal lifestyle. It's located in Oceania (Australia and New Zealand).
