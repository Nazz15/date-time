Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is India in?

India uses UTC+05:30. Based on its capital, New Delhi, this is 4 hours 30 minutes ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 9 hours 30 minutes ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does India observe Daylight Saving Time?

India does not observe Daylight Saving Time and has kept a single fixed UTC+05:30 offset nationwide since 1947. This affects how far ahead or behind India is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does India use?

India uses the Indian Rupee (INR, symbol ₹). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in India?

The official language of India is Hindi/English. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit India?

October through March when temperatures are cooler and humidity drops across most of the country. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in India, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit India?

most foreign visitors need an e-Visa applied for online before arrival; check eligibility and apply at least a few days ahead of travel. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with India's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is India known for?

India is known for the Taj Mahal, Bollywood cinema, the Himalayas, and a vast diversity of regional cuisines and festivals. It's located in Asia (Southern Asia).
