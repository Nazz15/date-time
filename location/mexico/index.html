Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Mexico in?

Mexico uses UTC-08:00, UTC-07:00, UTC-06:00, and UTC-05:00. Based on its capital, Mexico City, this is 7 hours behind London (GMT/BST) and 2 hours behind New York (EST/EDT). Note that Mexico spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Mexico City.

Does Mexico observe Daylight Saving Time?

Mexico does not observe Daylight Saving Time nationwide as of 2022, except in municipalities along the US border that align with US clock changes. This affects how far ahead or behind Mexico is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Mexico use?

Mexico uses the Mexican Peso (MXN, symbol $). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Mexico?

The official language of Mexico is Spanish. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Mexico?

October to April for the dry season and more comfortable temperatures across most of the country. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Mexico, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit Mexico?

citizens of the US, Canada, the EU, UK and many other countries can visit visa-free for up to 180 days with just a valid passport. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with Mexico's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is Mexico known for?

Mexico is known for Mayan and Aztec ruins, Caribbean beaches in Cancún, and vibrant street food and mariachi culture. It's located in Americas (North America).
