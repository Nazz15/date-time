Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Japan in?

Japan uses UTC+09:00. Based on its capital, Tokyo, this is 8 hours ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 13 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does Japan observe Daylight Saving Time?

Japan does not observe Daylight Saving Time and has kept a fixed UTC+09:00 offset since 1952. This affects how far ahead or behind Japan is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Japan use?

Japan uses the Japanese Yen (JPY, symbol ¥). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Japan?

The official language of Japan is Japanese. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Japan?

March to May for cherry blossoms or October to November for autumn colors, both with mild temperatures. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Japan, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit Japan?

What is Japan known for?

Japan is known for cherry blossom season, Mount Fuji, Tokyo's neon-lit districts, and centuries-old temples in Kyoto. It's located in Asia (Eastern Asia).
