Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is France in?

France uses UTC+01:00. Based on its capital, Paris, this is 1 hour ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 6 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does France observe Daylight Saving Time?

France observes Daylight Saving Time across the EU from late March to late October. This affects how far ahead or behind France is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does France use?

France uses the Euro (EUR, symbol €). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in France?

The official language of France is French. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit France?

April to June or September to October for pleasant weather and smaller crowds than peak summer. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in France, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit France?

citizens of the US, UK, Canada, Australia and other Schengen-exempt countries can visit visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period; other nationalities need a Schengen visa. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with France's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is France known for?

France is known for the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, French cuisine and wine regions like Bordeaux and Champagne. It's located in Europe (Western Europe).
