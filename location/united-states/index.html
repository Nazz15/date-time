Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is United States in?

United States uses UTC-12:00, UTC-11:00, UTC-10:00, UTC-09:00, UTC-08:00, UTC-07:00, UTC-06:00, UTC-05:00, and UTC+10:00. United States spans multiple timezones, so the figures above reflect Washington D.C..

Does United States observe Daylight Saving Time?

Most of the US observes Daylight Saving Time from March to November; Hawaii and most of Arizona do not change clocks.

What is the time difference between United States and London or New York?

Washington D.C. is 5 hours behind London (GMT/BST) and the same time as New York (EST/EDT). These gaps shift slightly across the year as other regions observe their own Daylight Saving Time changes.

What currency does United States use?

United States uses the US Dollar (USD, symbol $). Cards are widely accepted in major cities; carry some cash for smaller vendors.

What is the best time to visit United States?

April through June or September through October for milder weather and fewer crowds at major attractions. Conditions can vary significantly between regions, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit United States?

most visitors from Visa Waiver Program countries can travel for up to 90 days using ESTA instead of a visa; other nationalities need a B-2 tourist visa from a US embassy or consulate. Requirements vary by nationality, so confirm current rules with United States' official immigration authority before booking travel.
