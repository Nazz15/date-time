Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is United States in?

United States uses UTC-12:00, UTC-11:00, UTC-10:00, UTC-09:00, UTC-08:00, UTC-07:00, UTC-06:00, UTC-05:00, and UTC+10:00. Based on its capital, Washington D.C., this is 5 hours behind London (GMT/BST) and the same time as New York (EST/EDT). Note that United States spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Washington D.C..

Does United States observe Daylight Saving Time?

Most of the US observes Daylight Saving Time from March to November; Hawaii and most of Arizona do not change clocks. This affects how far ahead or behind United States is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does United States use?

United States uses the US Dollar (USD, symbol $). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in United States?

The official language of United States is English. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit United States?

April through June or September through October for milder weather and fewer crowds at major attractions. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in United States, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

Do I need a visa to visit United States?

most visitors from Visa Waiver Program countries can travel for up to 90 days using ESTA instead of a visa; other nationalities need a B-2 tourist visa from a US embassy or consulate. Requirements vary significantly by nationality, so confirm current rules with United States's official immigration authority or your nearest consulate before booking travel.

What is United States known for?

United States is known for its national parks, major cities like New York and Los Angeles, and a wide range of regional cultures across its 50 states. It's located in Americas (North America).
