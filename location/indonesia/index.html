About Indonesia

Indonesia spans 3 timezone(s) (UTC+07:00, UTC+08:00, and UTC+09:00), with its capital Jakarta located in Asia. The official currency is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) and the primary language is Indonesian. Indonesia's international dial code is +62.

Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is Indonesia in?

Indonesia uses UTC+07:00, UTC+08:00, and UTC+09:00. Based on its capital, Jakarta, this is 6 hours ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 11 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT). Note that Indonesia spans multiple timezones, so the time can vary by region — the offset above reflects Jakarta.

Does Indonesia observe Daylight Saving Time?

Indonesia does not observe Daylight Saving Time and uses three fixed time zones across its islands year-round. This affects how far ahead or behind Indonesia is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does Indonesia use?

Indonesia uses the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR, symbol Rp). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in Indonesia?

The official language of Indonesia is Indonesian. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit Indonesia?

April to October for the dry season, which is generally the most popular and reliable time to visit. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in Indonesia, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.

What is Indonesia known for?

Indonesia is known for Bali's beaches and temples, Komodo Island, and thousands of islands spanning diverse cultures and landscapes. It's located in Asia (South-Eastern Asia).
