Frequently Asked Questions

What timezone is South Africa in?

South Africa uses UTC+02:00. Based on its capital, Pretoria, this is 1 hour ahead of London (GMT/BST) and 6 hours ahead of New York (EST/EDT).

Does South Africa observe Daylight Saving Time?

South Africa does not observe Daylight Saving Time and keeps a fixed UTC+02:00 offset year-round. This affects how far ahead or behind South Africa is compared to other countries at different times of year.

What currency does South Africa use?

South Africa uses the South African Rand (ZAR, symbol R). Carry local currency for smaller vendors, though cards are widely accepted in major cities.

What language is spoken in South Africa?

The official language of South Africa is English/Zulu/Afrikaans. Visitors relying on English should check ahead, as prevalence varies between major cities and rural areas.

What is the best time to visit South Africa?

May to September for dry winter weather, which is also peak season for game viewing in national parks. Conditions can vary significantly between regions in South Africa, so check the specific city you're visiting for more precise guidance.
